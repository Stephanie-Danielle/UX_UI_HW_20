Collabify

Spotify's Collaborative Playlist Feature Update

Crystal Nava, Stephanie Cline, Yena Kim

Our team updated an existing feature on the most widely used music streaming app. We highlighted the collaborative playlist feature and created a more interactive user experience. As a user myself, I'm excited to share this fun feature with friends!

Adobe XD, InVision, Figma, Miro, Google Suite

Our Hypothesis: Changing the Spotify collaborative feature by making it easier to access, share, and collaborate will improve the user experience for music lovers who don’t feel satisfied with the current feature.

“People asked me to change the music, but I didn’t know what to play. It’s stressful!”

“I know Spotify has a collaborative playlist, but I never use it. It’s just not that great.”

“When the bluetooth speaker disconnects I get pissed, because it totally kills the mood.”

Based on the user interviews, we created an online survey to see how the numbers stack up.

62% of participants felt that collaborating on music playlists is inconvenient

72.7% of participants agreed that the interruption of music highly affects the ambience of a social gathering

77.3% of participants would like to add music to a party playlist if it were more convenient

What did our user research show us?

Difficult for hosts to create a playlist that everyone will enjoy

Music interruptions were a major pain point

Users want to shre music and collaborate seamlessly & easily

Problem Statement

Social music lovers like Camila need a collaborative playlist that is easily accessible and shareable, because they don’t want the ambience at social gathering ruined with disliked music or unnecessary pauses.
